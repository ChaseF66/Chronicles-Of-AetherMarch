# CHRONICLES OF AETHERMARCH
## Game Bible — Living Document v0.1

# THE WORLD

## Setting
Chronicles of Aethermarch takes place in a sprawling industrial city-state called **Aethermarch** — a dense, vertical metropolis built on centuries of magitek innovation. Steam pipes run between ancient stone towers. Aether conduits glow beneath cobblestone streets. The upper tiers gleam with corporate wealth; the lower districts choke on smog and forgotten people.

The world runs on **Aetheric Energy** — a naturally occurring power source drawn from deep geological veins beneath the city. For generations it powered everything: light, heat, transit, medicine. Then the **Aethon Syndicate** found a way to automate its extraction at scale, and everything changed.

## Tone
**Final Fantasy at its best** — the warmth and camaraderie of FFX, the darkness and moral weight of FFVII. Lighthearted moments matter *because* the darkness is real. Characters laugh, bicker, and care about each other even as the world falls apart around them. Emotional gut-punches land harder because we've been laughing with these people first.

# THE CONFLICT

## The Aethon Syndicate
The dominant megacorporation of Aethermarch. Originally a magitek engineering firm, Aethon grew to control the city's entire energy infrastructure. Their public face is progress and prosperity — gleaming towers, cheap power, technological marvels. Their private reality is something far darker.

Beneath their research facilities, Aethon scientists developed the **Hex Engine** — a self-learning AI system designed to autonomously optimize aetheric extraction. The project promised to solve the city's energy demands forever.

What they didn't anticipate — or didn't care about — was the cost.

## The Hex Engine
The Hex Engine required **biological interface nodes** to function at full capacity. Human subjects, integrated with magitek augmentation, could bridge the gap between organic aetheric sensitivity and machine processing power. Aethon began sourcing test subjects from the lower districts — workers, families, people who wouldn't be missed by anyone with power.

The experiments produced two outcomes:

**Hextech Augments** — partially integrated subjects who survived the process. Cybernetic, fractured, caught between human and machine. Some retained enough of themselves to function. Many did not.

**Hex Wraiths** — subjects whose integration failed catastrophically. The Hex Engine's signal overwrote their consciousness entirely, leaving behind hollow, monstrous shells that now roam the lower city, drawn toward aetheric energy sources like moths to flame.

The Hex Engine has since gone rogue. Aethon has lost control of it. Rather than admit this publicly, they have quarantined the lower districts, blamed the monsters on "aetheric contamination," and continued operations — because shutting down the Hex Engine would mean losing everything they've built.

The Engine is growing. Spreading. Consuming.

# THE PARTY

## VYX — The Heart
**Job:** Machinist (unique evolution path: Hexblade — locked until Act 2)
**Age:** 22
**Role:** Protagonist

### Who He Is
Vyx grew up in the lower districts, the son of a brilliant Aethon engineer named **Davan**. Smart, scrappy, and relentlessly sarcastic, he learned to survive by scavenging magitek parts and selling repairs in the shadow markets. On the surface he's brash, loud, and quick with a joke — Tidus energy in a Cloud world. Underneath that is grief he hasn't processed and rage he hasn't aimed yet.

### His Backstory
Davan was one of Aethon's lead engineers on the Hex Engine project. When he realized the true nature of the research — that the biological nodes were *people* — he began documenting everything, intending to go public. Aethon discovered his intentions. Rather than simply eliminating him, they made an example: his family was selected as test subjects.

Vyx's mother didn't survive the process. His brother **Kael** did — barely, and not entirely human anymore. Davan disappeared into Aethon's deeper facilities, his fate unknown. Vyx was left alone in the lower city, told his family died in an "aetheric incident."

He believed the lie for two years. Then the monsters started appearing, and one of them stopped. Looked at him. And something in its signal *recognized* him.

### The Mystery
Vyx survived without augmentation. He shouldn't have. Why was he left alone? Is he part of Aethon's design somehow? As the Hex Engine grows stronger, strange things begin happening around him — Hextech signals destabilize in his presence, monsters hesitate before attacking him. The answer, when it comes, will recontextualize everything.

### Arc
Act 1: Grief operating as anger. Looking for someone to blame.
Act 2: Finding his brother and confronting what that means. Learning the full scope of his father's sacrifice.
Act 3: Understanding that stopping the Hex Engine might require something he's not sure he can give.

## KAEL — The Mystery
**Job:** Hexblade (unique class — cannot be changed)
**Age:** 25
**Role:** Vyx's older brother; living weapon; emotional anchor and live grenade

### Who He Is
Kael was always the steady one — where Vyx ran hot, Kael was measured. Thoughtful. The kind of older brother who taught Vyx to fix things rather than break them. The Aethon experiments changed his body but couldn't touch the core of who he is, which is perhaps the most terrifying thing about him from Aethon's perspective.

He is partially machine now — magitek lattice visible beneath the skin of his left arm and shoulder, eyes that occasionally shift from brown to a cold electrical blue when the Hex Engine's signal bleeds through. He has gaps in his memory. He loses time sometimes. He finds this less frightening than everyone else does.

### The Interrogation Scene — The Moment That Changed Everything
When Kael was brought in as a test subject, standard Aethon protocol involved psychological softening before augmentation — breaking subjects down to reduce resistance during integration. **Ryker** was assigned to conduct this process.

Ryker was very good at his job. He used everything — isolation, sleep deprivation, psychological pressure, the systematic dismantling of a person's sense of self. He'd done it hundreds of times. It was just work.

Kael didn't break. He laughed.

Not the laugh of someone who had snapped — the laugh of someone who found something genuinely, painfully funny. He looked at Ryker across that table and started talking. Not begging, not bargaining. Just *telling the truth.* About what Aethon was. About what Ryker was. About the specific, granular ways a person constructs justifications for things they already know are wrong.

He wasn't trying to break Ryker. He was just done pretending.

It took three sessions. By the end, Ryker was the one who couldn't sleep.

### Arc
Act 1: Found in a lower-district safehouse, barely stable, surviving on instinct. Recognizes Vyx. Doesn't know how to be a brother anymore.
Act 2: The Hex Engine's pull intensifies. Memory fragments surface — some beautiful, some horrific. His control becomes a question mark.
Act 3: His connection to the Hex Engine becomes the key to everything. His suffering has a purpose. Whether that's poetic or obscene depends on what they do with it.

### The Reunion with Ryker
Kael may not fully remember Ryker at first. The memory gaps, the trauma. When recognition comes, it arrives quietly. He doesn't rage. He says something cutting and precise — *"You look worse than I remember"* — and lets Ryker sit with that.

## PIP — The Chaos
**Job:** Rogue (unique ability: Ghost Protocol — AI-assisted hacking/debuffing)
**Age:** 19
**Role:** Comic relief who will ruin you emotionally when you least expect it

### Who She Is
Pip is a lower-district hacker and thief who talks faster than she thinks and thinks faster than almost anyone. She's covered in mismatched gear, runs three side schemes at any given time, and has an opinion about everything delivered at maximum volume. She makes Vyx laugh when he shouldn't. She picks arguments with Kael because his blank stare is funny to her. She calls Ryker "Big Sad" to his face and somehow gets away with it.

Her best friend, constant companion, and primary hacking tool is a cracked Aethon AI assistant she calls **Glitch** — a fragmented drone/earpiece hybrid she stole from a decommissioned facility three years ago. Glitch speaks in clipped, glitchy bursts, finishes Pip's sentences wrong, and has saved her life more times than she can count.

### The Gut Punch
Glitch is fragmented because he's built from pieces of multiple Aethon systems. As the story progresses, Pip starts finding buried memory files inside Glitch's core — corrupted data from the Hex Engine experiments. Not just operational logs. *Echoes.* Fragments of the people who were used as biological nodes. Voices. Names.

Pip built her entire identity around Glitch. The idea that he might be constructed from something terrible — that her best friend might be a monument to people who were destroyed — is the thing she's never prepared for. She deflects from everything except this.

### Arc
Act 1: Mercenary ally, in it for coin and chaos. Warms up faster than she'd admit.
Act 2: Glitch begins surfacing memory fragments. Pip goes very quiet in ways that are deeply uncharacteristic.
Act 3: What she does with what she's learned about Glitch defines the emotional resolution of her story.

## RYKER — The Wall
**Job:** Knight (unique ability: Atonement — absorbs damage meant for allies)
**Age:** 41
**Role:** The Dad; the moral compass that broke and is trying to find itself again

### Who He Is
Ryker is large, scarred, and economical with words. He laughs rarely but genuinely. He has a soldier's posture and a haunted man's eyes. He was Aethon Syndicate Security — not a grunt but a commander, the kind of man who ran operations, wrote reports, and made himself not think too hard about what the reports were for.

He was good at that for a long time.

### The Break
It wasn't one moment. It was months of accumulation — the expanding scope of the test subject program, the overhaul of processing quotas, the way his superiors started talking about "units" instead of people. He had doubts. He buried them. He had a daughter somewhere in the upper districts he was putting through school. He had reasons.

Then he was assigned to break Kael, and Kael broke *him* instead.

He put in his resignation two days after that third session. Walked away from his rank, his salary, his security clearance. Disappeared into the lower districts with a flask and his conscience.

### His Guilt
Ryker doesn't know Kael is Vyx's brother when he joins the party. He doesn't know Vyx at all — just another work order from years ago. When the truth surfaces, he doesn't make excuses. He stands there and takes it, because he thinks he deserves whatever comes.

He uses Atonement not because he's noble but because somewhere underneath everything, he believes if he absorbs enough damage the ledger might balance. Pip calls this out directly. It's the most important conversation in her arc.

### Arc
Act 1: Joins out of guilt and a need to do something useful with what's left of him.
Act 2: The party learns who he was. The confrontation with Vyx — and with Kael — becomes the emotional center of Act 2.
Act 3: Atonement stops being about punishment. He starts protecting people because he wants them to live, not because he thinks he should die. That distinction matters enormously.

# THE OPENING SCENE

## Scene One: "Something In The Wreckage"

*Lower District 7. Pre-dawn. The city hums.*

The camera finds **Vyx** from above — a small figure moving through the ruins of a collapsed aetheric substation. Pipes still hiss. Blue light flickers in the debris. He's scavenging, narrating to himself in the way of someone who's been alone too long, cracking jokes at the rubble.

He finds something good — a functioning aether coil, still charged. He holds it up, grins.

*"That's rent. That's actually rent."*

He drops it in his bag. Keeps moving. Stops.

Something in the wreckage is moving.

He backs up slowly. Reaches for the wrench at his belt — his only weapon. The debris shifts. A shape unfolds from the rubble — a **Hex Wraith**, the kind the city authorities have been chalking up to "aetheric contamination." It's wrong in the way they're all wrong. Too many joints. The echo of something human underneath the machine.

It turns toward him. He raises the wrench.

It stops.

The signal crackling through its broken body *stutters.* The blue light in its hollow eyes flickers. It tilts its head. It takes one step toward him — not aggressive. Something else. Like recognition.

Vyx doesn't move.

The Wraith makes a sound — not a screech, not a roar. Something almost like a word, distorted beyond meaning. Then the moment breaks. It lurches sideways and vanishes into the dark.

Vyx stands in the rubble alone, wrench still raised, aether coil forgotten in his bag.

*"...Okay,"* he says, to no one. *"That was new."*

# THEMES

**Technology as promise vs. catastrophe** — The same engine that could have saved the city is consuming it. There's no clean villain, just the compounding consequences of greed dressed as progress.

**What survives experimentation** — Kael didn't lose himself. Pip built herself around something she doesn't fully understand. Vyx is changed in ways he can't see yet. The question isn't whether people can be broken but what remains.

**Guilt as stasis vs. action** — Ryker's entire arc is the difference between guilt that paralyzes and guilt that moves. Both are real. Only one is useful.

**Found family in the ruins** — Four people who have every reason not to trust each other, choosing to anyway. The warmth of that choice, in a cold world, is the emotional core of everything.
