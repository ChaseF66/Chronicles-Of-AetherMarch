## PART ONE: THE STREETS BEFORE SUNRISE

---

[SCENE]
*Vyx emerges from the collapsed substation into the pre-dawn streets of Lower District 7. The air is thick — not just with smog, but with something heavier. Absence. The street that should be waking up isn't. Shuttered windows. A food cart abandoned mid-setup, canvas still tied down. A child's shoe in the middle of the road.*
*The aether conduits beneath the cobblestones still pulse their faint blue. The city's veins, keeping time regardless.*

[SOUND]
*Distant pressure hiss from a cracked pipe somewhere. Wind moving through empty streets. The occasional flicker of a dying light fixture overhead.*

[VYX]
*"Three weeks ago this block had forty families on it."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*He walks slowly. Taking it in. His eyes move over the details — a quarantine notice half-torn from a doorframe, the symbol underneath scratched out with something sharp. A window on the second floor, curtain moving slightly. Someone still here. Someone still watching.*

[VYX]
*"Syndicate called it a voluntary evacuation. Put it in the official report and everything."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Nobody volunteered."*

[SCENE]
*He passes a wall covered in names. Handwritten, in different colors of paint, different hands. Some neat. Some desperate. Some with dates beneath them. He slows. Doesn't stop. He's seen it before. That doesn't make it easier.*

[NARRATION]
*The wall has been here for two months. It gets longer every week.*

*(beat)*

[VYX — quietly, not really to himself, not really to anyone]*
*"Hey, Mrs. Orveil. Still got that pot you lent me."*

*(beat — long)*

[SCENE]
*He keeps walking. Passes a Syndicate monitoring tower — automated, no human operator. Its sensor array sweeps the street in slow arcs. Red light. Red light. Red light.*
*Vyx steps into a doorway without thinking. Pure reflex. Waits for the sweep to pass.*

[VYX]
*"Yeah. Still doing that. Two years later, still doing that."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*The sweep passes. He steps back out. Looks up at the tower for a long moment — something behind his eyes that isn't quite anger and isn't quite grief. Something that's been living in the space between those two things for a very long time.*

[VYX]
*"Dad used to say the Syndicate wasn't evil. Just... indifferent. Said indifference at scale was worse than malice."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"I used to argue with him about that."*

*(beat)*

[VYX — almost a whisper]*
*"I don't anymore."*

[SCENE]
*He turns a corner. His workshop is two blocks ahead — a converted storage unit on the ground floor of a half-collapsed building. Home, more or less. The aether coil in his bag presses against his back. Rent. Actually rent.*
*For just a moment, the weight lifts a little. He almost smiles.*

[VYX]
*"Okay. Two blocks. Don't get weird, don't get—"*

---

## PART TWO: THE AMBUSH

---

*(hard cut)*

[SCENE]
*Something slams into him from the left.*
*Not a tackle — a strike. Magitek-enhanced, fast, wrong. He hits the cobblestones hard, bag skidding away. The aether coil rolls out across the street.*
*He's already rolling, already on his feet — the survival instinct of someone who's been living alone in a quarantine zone for two years. Wrench in hand before he's fully upright.*

[SOUND]
*That crackling static. Loud now. Close. Filling the street.*

[SCENE]
*A HEX WRAITH stands between him and his workshop.*
*Different from the one in the substation. That one paused. Flickered. This one has no hesitation — it's already moving, already orienting, signal burning at full intensity. The hollow blue eyes don't recognize anything. There's nothing left in there to recognize.*

[VYX]
*"Okay. OKAY."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Don't panic. You've seen these. You know how they work. Probably."*

[SCENE]
*The Wraith collapses. Its signal spikes once — violent, desperate — then flatlines. The blue light in its eyes dims to nothing. The magitek lattice goes dark piece by piece, like lights going out in a building.*
*Vyx stands over it. Wrench raised. Waiting.*
*It doesn't get back up.*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Okay."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Okay. Yeah. That's — yeah."*

[SCENE]
*He lowers the wrench. Looks at his hand. Still not shaking. He's not sure if that's good or bad anymore. He picks up the aether coil from the cobblestones — still intact, somehow — and just holds it for a moment.*

[VYX — quietly]*
*"Still rent."*

*(beat — long)*

## PART FIVE: THE WATCHER

---

[SOUND]
*A slow, deliberate clap. Three beats. From above.*

[SCENE]
*Vyx spins, wrench up.*
*PIP is sitting on the rusted railing of a fire escape one floor up, legs dangling, completely unbothered. She's seventeen layers of mismatched gear and sharp eyes and she's been up there long enough to get comfortable. A small drone hovers near her shoulder — cracked casing, one eye flickering — watching Vyx with what seems like genuine curiosity.*
*She takes a bite of something.*

[PIP]
*"Four out of ten on form. Seven out of ten on result. I'll average it to a five and call it passing."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"...How long have you been up there."*

[PIP]
*"Since before the Wraith showed up."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"You watched the whole thing."*

[PIP]
*"Yep."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"And you didn't help."*

[PIP]
*"Nope."*

*(beat — long)*

[VYX]
*"I could have DIED."*

[PIP]
*"You could have! But I didn't think you would. And I was right, so."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — quieter, the easy confidence still there but something underneath it now]*
*"Look. I've watched a lot of people in this district try to fight one of those things. Most of them I couldn't help even if I wanted to. The ones I could have helped—"*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I learned a long time ago not to bet on someone until I know they're worth betting on."*

*(beat — long)*

[PIP — back to easy, the door closing again]*
*"You're worth betting on. Congratulations. It's a very exclusive club, current membership: you and Glitch."*

*The drone makes a sound — somewhere between a beep and an offended chirp.*

[PIP]
*"Glitch says hi."*

*(beat)*

[VYX — lowering the wrench slowly, staring at her]*
*"...Who are you."*

[PIP]
*"Pip. And before you ask — yes I've been watching you for three weeks, yes I already left something at your workshop, and yes I know about the Wraith in the substation. The one that stopped."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"That's actually why we need to talk."*

## PART SIX: THE MESSAGE

---

[SCENE]
*Vyx's workshop. He unlocks the heavy door, steps inside — and immediately sees the magitek transmitter on his workbench. Small, handbuilt, blinking. Not his.*

[VYX]
*"You broke into my workshop."*

[PIP — from behind him, already inside somehow]*
*"I prefer 'let myself in.' The lock is very basic, by the way, you should upgrade."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"How are you already—"*

[PIP]
*"Faster route. Do you want to discuss my commute or do you want to hear what I found?"*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*She pulls up a holographic display from Glitch — the drone projecting fragmented data maps, signal readings, six months of collected observations. It's sloppy and brilliant at the same time, held together with stolen code and sheer stubbornness.*

[PIP]
*"Six months of Hex Wraith behavior data. Every scan Glitch can run. And in six months I have never — not once — seen one of those things stop. Recognize someone. Hesitate."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"Until this morning."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — looking at him directly now, the fast-talk gone, something genuinely serious underneath]*
*"So I need you to tell me everything. Because either something is changing in the Hex Engine's signal — which is very bad — or something about you is interfering with it."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"And I genuinely cannot decide which one scares me more."*

*(beat — long)*

[VYX — looking at the data, then at her]*
*"...I don't know what it was."*

[PIP]
*"I know. That's why we're going to find out."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Glitch chirps. The data maps flicker. Outside, somewhere in the lower district, another signal pulses — faint, distant, searching.*
*Neither of them hear it.*
*Not yet.*
