---

> **TONE NOTE:**
> This is the first time the game breathes.
> Everything before this was District 7 — compressed, contaminated, survival.
> Scene 04 is the blindfold coming off.
> The world is bigger than District 7. Fuller. Stranger. More beautiful
> and more broken than anything the smog allowed them to see.
> We see it through Vyx's eyes — someone who has been in a box for two years
> suddenly standing in front of the full painting for the first time.
> What Vyx sees becomes the world.
> What Glitch files becomes the record.
> Let it breathe. Let it land. Don't rush a single second of it.

---

> **GLITCH NOTE:**
> Everything Glitch scans and files in this scene is marked [GLITCH — FILING].
> These are not throwaway comedy beats.
> These are the entries in an archive that will matter later.

> He's always known.
> He just hasn't been asked.

# PART ONE: THE LAST WALL

[SCENE]
*The outer edge of District 7.*

*The buildings thin. The infrastructure loosens its grip.
The density that has defined every breath, every step, every waking moment
for two years — begins, slowly, to let go.*

*And the air changes.*

*Not clean. Not yet. Maybe not for a long time.
But different. Like the difference between a room that has been closed
for years and a room where someone just cracked a window.*

*Vyx notices.*

*His body notices before his mind does —
his lungs taking a slightly fuller breath without being asked,
his shoulders dropping a fraction without permission,
two years of compressed survival instinct loosening
one molecule at a time.*

*He doesn't say anything.*

*He just walks slightly slower.*

*Pip notices him noticing.*
*Doesn't say anything either.*

*Behind them both —*

*[GLITCH — FILING]*
*Scans the transition zone between urban district and industrial corridor.*

*Files it under: ENVIRONMENTAL — DISTRICT BOUNDARY — ZONE TRANSITION.*
*Moves on.*

[SCENE]
*The last building of District 7.*

*Twelve stories. Residential. Mostly dark —
but not entirely. A few windows still lit from inside.

[SCENE]
*The industrial corridor begins.*

*And Vyx stops walking.*

*Not from danger. Not from exhaustion.*

*From the sheer fact of what's in front of him.*

*The aether pipelines.*

*He knew they existed — everyone in District 7 knows they exist,
the hum of them is the background frequency of every day,
the infrastructure that carries processed aether
from the lower district facilities to the central Hex Engine network.
He knew they were large.*

*He did not know they were THIS.*

*Enormous. Wider than streets. Wider than buildings.
Running east in parallel lines like the bones of some
creature so massive it stopped being a creature
and became geography instead.
Still humming. Still carrying.
The pipeline percussion he has heard his whole life —
suddenly here, suddenly physical, suddenly something
you feel in your chest rather than just hear in the background.*

*And the light.*

*Two years of no maintenance on joints rated for five-year intervals.
Basic material fatigue — the seals degraded, cracked, gave way.
And through those cracks: raw aether.*

*Bleeding.*

*Thin luminous streams of blue-green light
drifting upward from every compromised joint,
curling in the cold air above the pipeline,
dissipating slowly into the dark like the world
is exhaling something it's been holding too long.*

*Beautiful.*

*Toxic.*

*Both.*

*Vyx stands in the middle of it and turns slowly.
Taking it in. The scale of it. The strangeness of it.
The fact that this has been here, this whole time,
just outside the edge of everything he knew.*

[VYX — very quietly, to himself]*
*"...How did I not know it looked like this."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — watching him, something fond in it]*
*"You couldn't see it from inside."*

*(beat)*

# PART TWO: THE ENCOUNTER

---

> **DESIGN NOTE:**
> The creature here is an AETHER-CORRUPTED MAINTENANCE DRONE.
> Not a wraith. Not a monster. Something sadder than that.
> A pipeline unit that absorbed two years of raw aether bleed
> and became something it was never designed to be.
> It's not malicious. It's confused.
> The combat is SHORT — two or three turns maximum.
> The point isn't the fight.
> The point is HOW they fight together for the first time.
> Without a plan. Just instinct. See how they fit.

---
