# CHRONICLES OF AETHERMARCH
## Character Design Document — VYX
### "The Heart"
### Version 1.0

---

# OVERVIEW

Vyx is the player. The lens. The person standing in the wreckage of a world that broke and deciding to push back anyway.

His design should communicate everything about who he is before he says a word. When you see his silhouette — tall, lean, slightly worn — you should feel:

- Scrappy. Resourceful.
- Tired but not beaten.
- Young but carrying something older.
- Someone who fixes things. Who uses his hands.
- Someone you'd follow into a burning building because you believe he'd find a way out.

---

# PHYSICAL PROFILE

## Base Build
**Height:** 6'2"
**Weight:** Approximately 185-190 lbs — naturally athletic frame carved lean by two years of survival
**Age:** 22
**Ethnicity/Appearance:** Based on Chase Fingulin — tall, mixed heritage, strong jaw, expressive face

## The Survival Effect
Vyx's natural frame is bigger than what two years of District 7 has left him with. He should look like someone who WAS built heavier — you can see the potential in his shoulders, in his hands, in the way he carries himself. But survival doesn't leave room for maintaining that. He's leaner than he should be. Not unhealthy. Just... used up in the specific way of someone who burns everything he has every day just to keep going.

**What this looks like:**
- Broad shoulders that have thinned slightly
- Arms that are defined from work, not bulk
- A jaw that's sharper than it used to be
- Eyes that are older than 22

## The Silhouette
Standing still: Tall. Slightly hunched — not from weakness, from habit. The habit of moving through tight spaces, of keeping his head down under surveillance sweeps, of two years of making himself smaller than he is.

Moving: Completely different. Efficient. Purposeful. Every movement has a reason. No wasted motion. The specific economy of someone who learned to move through dangerous spaces without making noise.

When he's fighting: He opens up. The full height. The full reach. The wrench becomes an extension of him rather than a tool in his hand. THIS is the version of Vyx that surprises people.

# COSTUME DESIGN

## The Philosophy
Everything Vyx wears was either salvaged, repaired, or stolen in the nicest possible way. Nothing was bought. Nothing was given to him. Every piece of his outfit has a story and most of those stories involve him being very resourceful in a very difficult situation.

The color palette runs through everything: **dark base tones with blue and toxic green accents.** Not by design — by proximity. Two years of living in aether-saturated District 7 has left traces of his environment on everything he owns. The blue-green tint in his materials is District 7 on him. Literally.

## The Jacket
The centerpiece. A heavy work jacket — originally dark grey, now closer to dark brown-grey from years of grime and repair. Long enough to hit mid-thigh. Built for utility rather than style, though time and Vyx's own modifications have given it a style anyway.

**Details:**
- Multiple tool loops across the chest and shoulders — most of them full. Screwdrivers, small hex tools, wire cutters, things that don't have names but have purposes.
- Patches covering repairs — different fabrics, different textures, none of them matching. Each patch is a story. A tear from a Wraith encounter. A burn from a conduit bleed. A cut from something that doesn't need to be discussed.
- The patch materials have absorbed aether — they have that faint blue-green tint. Salvaged from pipeline insulation, from conduit housing, from things adjacent to the aether network.
- Collar turned up — not for style. For the monitoring tower sweeps. Old habit.
- Left sleeve has more patches than the right — that's the arm he leads with when he's working.
- Inside pocket, chest left — where the ring goes when he's working and can't wear it. He always puts it back.

## The Shirt Underneath
Dark. Simple. Worn thin in places. The kind of shirt that used to be one color and is now another color from washing in whatever water was available.

## The Pants
Dark work pants. Reinforced at the knees — he spends a lot of time crouching, climbing, kneeling in rubble. Cargo pockets on both thighs, both full. Belt is functional — loops hold small tools and the wrench when it's not in his hand.

## The Boots
The most important piece. Vyx's boots are the thing he maintains most carefully because everything else can be improvised but bad boots will get you killed in District 7. Dark leather, steel-toed, resoled twice that he can count. Worn smooth on the outside from the cobblestones. The soles have absorbed enough aether from two years of District 7's streets that they have a faint blue glow — barely visible, only in darkness. He noticed once and decided not to think about it.

## The Wrench
Not a costume element — an extension of him. Medium-sized, heavy, the kind of wrench that was built for serious industrial work. Worn smooth where his hand always grips it. Modified — the handle wrapped in blue-green cord salvaged from pipeline insulation. The head has seen enough use that it's slightly misshapen on one side. He's never replaced it. He's thought about it. He hasn't.

It hangs at his belt on the right side. Always accessible. Always there.

## The Ring
His father's. Simple band, worn gold, clearly old. He wears it on his right hand, middle finger. When he's working — anything that requires both hands — he takes it off and puts it in the inside chest pocket of the jacket.

He has never lost it.

He has come close twice.

The ring is the warmest color on him. The only warm color on him. It stands out against everything else he wears like a small light in a dark room.

# FACE & HAIR

## Face
Strong jaw. Expressive — his face does the work his voice sometimes doesn't. When he's being sarcastic (often) it's in his eyes before it's in his words. When he's in pain (often) he thinks he's hiding it. He isn't.

Two years of surviving have sharpened his features slightly — the jaw a little more defined, the cheekbones a little more visible, the eyes carrying something that wasn't there before the collapse.

Light scarring — small, from two years of close calls. Nothing dramatic. Just the specific texture of a face that's been near things that were dangerous.

## Eyes
Dark. Expressive. Catch the light slightly blue even in normal state — always have, he never noticed. The people who know what to look for notice immediately.

In flow state: Blue-green glow. Not subtle.

## Hair
Dark. Natural. The kind of hair that does what it wants and he stopped fighting it. Not styled — managed. Kept out of his face because he can't afford to have his vision blocked. Longer than it used to be — he cuts it himself when it becomes a problem and not before.

---

# THE DETAILS THAT TELL HIS STORY

**His hands:** Calloused. Scarred in small ways. The specific wear of someone who uses them constantly for work. Blue-green aether residue that doesn't fully wash out anymore — it's in the skin now.

**His posture when still:** Slightly compressed. The habit of two years of making himself smaller than he is. Standing under surveillance sweeps. Moving through tight spaces.

**His posture when moving:** Opens up completely. Full height. The compression disappears. This surprises people who meet him standing still.

**His posture when fighting:** Complete. Full reach. Full presence. This is the version of Vyx people don't see coming.

**The way he holds the wrench:** Like it grew there. Not gripped — held. The difference between holding something you picked up and holding something that belongs in your hand.

---

# THE AETHER VISUAL

## Normal State
Subtle. Easy to miss. The faint blue caught in his eyes. The slight tint in his jacket patches. The glow in the soles of his boots in darkness. The residue on his hands that doesn't wash out.

He's been soaking in aether for two years. It shows. Just quietly.

## Flow State
The blue-green ignites. Not subtle anymore.

Aura: Ethereal blue bleeding into toxic green. Pulsing — not steady, not smooth. Like a signal with interference. Like something trying to be pure and carrying the evidence of where it's been.

Eyes: Full blue-green. The faint catch of light becomes complete glow.

Movement: Afterimages in blue-green trail behind him as he moves. The specific visual of someone moving faster than the light can quite track.

The crash: Colors fade from outside in. Green first, then blue. He goes to one knee. Hands on the ground. Breathing hard. Looks at his hands — they're still glowing faintly, fading. He watches until they're normal again. Every time.

# DESIGN NOTES FOR ARTISTS

**The silhouette test:** Vyx should be immediately recognizable as a black shape against white. The height. The collar up. The wrench at his belt. The slight compression of his posture. That's him.

**The contrast principle:** Everything about him is muted and worn EXCEPT the ring (warm gold) and the aether traces (blue-green). Those two things should always read clearly against the dark base palette.

**The evolution:** Early game Vyx looks tired. Worn. The compression in his posture is prominent. As the story progresses — as he finds his brother, understands his lineage, discovers his power — the posture opens. Slowly. By Act 3 he stands at full height naturally. The change should be subtle enough that players might not consciously notice it but will feel it.

**The ring:** Always visible when his right hand is shown. Always. It is never forgotten. It is never missing. It is the most important detail on him and it should be treated that way.

---

# REFERENCE ENERGY

**Body type:** Athletic-lean. Think a 22-year-old who was built bigger and survival carved it down. Functional muscle. Nothing wasted.

**Movement energy:** Efficient. Economic. No wasted motion. When still — compressed. When moving — opened up.

**Facial energy:** Expressive. The sarcasm lives in his eyes. The grief lives there too, underneath, always.

**Overall impression:** You look at Vyx and think: tired. Then he moves and you think: dangerous. Then he talks and you think: I'd follow him.

That sequence. In that order. Every time.
