# CHRONICLES OF AETHERMARCH
## Scene — "His Father's Work"
### The Road East | Day Two | Night
### The Aether Tutorial & The Second Gut Punch
### Version 1.0

---

> This scene takes place on the second night of the road east.
> The smog has thinned. A few stars visible through breaks in the ceiling.
> They've stopped to rest near an above-ground aether pipeline —
> its degraded seals weeping slow blue light into the air around them.
> Glitch is recharging. Quiet for once.
> This is the first real quiet they've had since District 7.

---

## PART ONE: THE LESSON

---

[SCENE]
*Night on the road east. The massive aether pipeline runs alongside their camp — its joints bleeding thin streams of blue light that drift upward and dissipate in the dark air above. Beautiful. Toxic. Both.*

*Pip is crouched near one of the bleeds, studying it with the focused attention she gives everything she finds genuinely interesting. Glitch rests nearby — systems cycling quietly, that flickering eye dimmed to almost nothing. Recharging.*

*Vyx sits a few feet away, watching the light drift. He hasn't said much since they stopped. The open ground has done something to him — the space, the sky, the tower visible from outside for the first time. He's still processing.*

[PIP]
*"You've been staring at that pipeline bleed for twenty minutes."*

[VYX]
*"It's doing something."*

[PIP]
*"It's leaking. Two years of no maintenance on joints rated for five-year intervals. Basic material fatigue."*

[VYX]
*"That's not what I mean."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Pip looks at him. Then at the bleed. Then back at him.*

[PIP]
*"...You can feel it."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Is that weird?"*

[PIP — standing, brushing off her hands, something shifting in her focus]*
*"No. It's actually— no. Come here."*

---

[SCENE]
*She shows him.*

*Not how she learned it — which was careful, methodical, built up from theoretical framework before any practical application. She learned it like chemistry. Like engineering. Step by step, safety first, understand the mechanism before you touch it.*

*She shows him differently. Because she can already tell he doesn't need the framework. He needs permission.*

[PIP]
*"Raw aether isn't like processed. Processed is Aethon's version — refined, calibrated, safe for general use. This—"*

*She gestures at the bleed.*

[PIP]
*"This is what it actually is. Before they get to it. Don't think of it as energy. Think of it as... language. It's trying to say something. You're just learning to listen."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"That's not very scientific."*

[PIP]
*"No. But it's accurate."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*She demonstrates. Cups her hands near the bleed — not touching it, just near it. The drifting light responds. Subtle. Almost imperceptible. But it curves slightly toward her hands instead of drifting randomly upward.*

*Vyx watches.*

[PIP]
*"Your turn."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"What do I—"*

[PIP]
*"Don't think about it. Just... listen."*

---

[SCENE]
*Vyx holds his hands near the bleed.*

*For a moment — nothing.*

*Then:*

*The light moves. Not subtly. Not imperceptibly. It MOVES — curves toward his hands with a certainty that has nothing to do with proximity, pools in the space between his palms like it's been waiting for somewhere to go.*

*Vyx stares at it.*

*Pip stares at it.*

*(beat — long)*

[VYX — very quietly]*
*"...Is that supposed to happen like that?"*

*(beat)*

[PIP — her voice carefully controlled]*
*"No."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I mean— it's not bad. It's just— that took me three weeks."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Maybe you're a bad teacher."*

[PIP]
*"I am an excellent teacher. That was not a teaching thing. That was a YOU thing."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*The aether light pulses gently between his palms. Warm. Alive in a way that processed aether never feels. He turns his hands slowly, watching it move with him. Instinctive. Natural.*

*Like breathing.*

*Like something he's been doing his whole life without knowing what to call it.*

[VYX — quietly, not really to Pip]*
*"My dad used to—"*

*(beat)*

*He stops.*

[VYX]
*"...Nothing. Never mind."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — watching him carefully]*
*"Your dad used to what?"*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"He worked with aether. For Aethon. Engineering. He used to come home and there'd be this... residue on his hands. This blue light. He'd show me and Kael and we'd try to make it move and we couldn't. We were kids."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"I always thought it was just because we were kids."*

*(beat)*

---

## PART TWO: THE NAME

---

[SCENE]
*Pip is quiet for a moment. Processing. She does this — goes internal when something catches on something else inside her, when data is cross-referencing against data.*

*Vyx doesn't notice. He's still watching the light between his palms.*

[PIP — carefully, like she's approaching something she doesn't want to startle]*
*"Your dad was an Aethon engineer."*

[VYX]
*"Lead engineer. On the Hex Engine project. Before he—"*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Before he disappeared."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"What was his area? His specific research focus?"*

[VYX]
*"Aetheric interface systems. The bridge between organic sensitivity and machine processing. He was trying to—"*

*(beat)*

*Something in Pip's stillness.*

[VYX]
*"What."*

[PIP]
*"Nothing. Keep going."*

[VYX]
*"Pip."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — the carefully controlled voice again]*
*"When I was researching Aethon. On the dark web. Looking for anything I could find about the collapse, about the experiments, about what they were actually doing—"*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I found research notes. Published fragments, mostly — things that got out before Aethon's suppression teams could scrub them. Some of it was clearly classified material that leaked through channels they didn't catch in time."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"There was one researcher whose work kept appearing. Everywhere. His framework for raw aether interaction was— I've never seen anything like it. The way he described the relationship between human biology and aetheric frequency, the theoretical basis for direct attunement without processing—"*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"That's what I learned from. That's how I learned to do what I just showed you. His notes."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — very quietly]*
*"The fragments were unsigned. But there was a name in some of the older documents. Buried in citations, in acknowledgment sections, in the metadata of files that Aethon almost scrubbed completely."*

*(beat)*

*She looks at him.*

[PIP]
*"What was your father's name, Vyx."*

*(beat — long)*

*(beat)*

*(beat)*

[VYX — already knowing, already feeling the ground shift beneath him]*
*"Say that name again."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"...What?"*

[VYX — quieter, raw]*
*"The name in the documents. Say it."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"Davan."*

*(beat — the longest one)*

[SCENE]
*The aether light between Vyx's palms flickers.*

*He doesn't notice.*

*He's somewhere else entirely.*

---

[SCENE]
*When he comes back — it takes a moment — his voice is completely level. Which is somehow worse than if it wasn't.*

[VYX]
*"That's my father."*

*(beat)*

[PIP — barely audible]*
*"...Oh."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Pip sits down. Actually sits down — not crouching, not strategic, just sits down on the ground next to the pipeline like her legs made a decision without asking her.*

*She looks at her hands. The hands she learned raw aether with. The hands that practiced his framework for months. The hands that just passed that framework to his son.*

*(beat)*

[PIP — very quietly]*
*"I've been carrying his work for two years."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Yeah."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I didn't know. I had no idea he had a— I never connected—"*

[VYX]
*"I know."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"Vyx."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"His notes. The way he wrote about aether — about what it actually is, about what people could do with it if Aethon's processing was removed from the equation — he understood it at a level that nobody else did. Nobody I've ever read. He wasn't just an engineer. He was—"*

*(beat)*

[PIP — choosing the word carefully]*
*"He was trying to give it back. The access. The direct connection. He wanted people to have what your family has naturally. That's what the research was FOR."*

*(beat)*

*Vyx looks at his hands. The aether light has faded — he stopped paying attention to it and it drifted away. Just light again. Just a pipeline bleed in the dark.*

*(beat)*

[VYX — quietly, and this is the most devastated he's sounded since "That's my brother"]*
*"He was trying to free people from Aethon."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"And they took him for it."*

*(beat — long, held)*

[SCENE]
*Neither of them says anything for a while.*

*The pipeline hums. The aether light bleeds upward into the dark. Somewhere above the smog, through a break in the ceiling, three stars are visible.*

*Glitch stirs. His eye brightens slightly. He processes something — a fragment of signal static from the corridor, nothing significant — and dims again.*

*Pip watches him.*

*(beat)*

[PIP — very quietly, not looking at Vyx]*
*"For what it's worth."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"His framework. The way he wrote about it. You could feel how much he loved it. The work. The understanding. He wasn't writing for Aethon. He was writing for—"*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I don't know. For whoever found it, I think. For whoever needed it."*

*(beat)*

[PIP]
*"I needed it. And now you—"*

*(beat)*

*She stops.*

[VYX — finishing it, quietly]*
*"And now I have it back."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Vyx closes his hand. Opens it again. Holds it near the pipeline bleed.*

*The light moves. Immediately. Certainly. His.*

*He looks at it for a long time.*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"Thank you."*

*(beat)*

*Pip doesn't say you're welcome. She just nods.*

*Some things don't need more words than that.*

## PART THREE: WHAT GLITCH HEARD

---

[SCENE]
*Later. Much later. Pip asleep. Vyx keeping watch.*

*Glitch drifts over. His eye is fully bright now — systems recharged, processing humming. He hovers near Vyx and does what he always does: scans him. From different angles. Unnecessarily.*

[VYX — quietly, not wanting to wake Pip]*
*"I know you heard all of that."*

*Glitch beeps.*

[VYX]
*"Don't tell me you didn't. You were 'recharging' with one eye open the whole time."*

*Glitch makes a sound that might be sheepish.*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"She didn't know. She wasn't hiding it from me."*

*Glitch beeps once. Soft. Understood.*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"My dad's out there somewhere. His work is out there. His... whatever this is—"*

*He holds up his hand. The light from the pipeline moves toward it without him trying.*

[VYX]
*"He put it somewhere people could find it. On purpose. Even knowing what Aethon would do to him for it."*

*(beat)*

[VYX]
*"That's either the bravest thing I've ever heard or the stupidest."*

*Glitch beeps.*

[VYX]
*"Yeah. Me too."*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Glitch hovers there for a moment. Then does something unexpected — drifts close and just... stays. Not scanning. Not analyzing. Just present.*

*Vyx looks at him.*

*(beat)*

[VYX — quietly]*
*"You're not so bad, you know that?"*

*Glitch makes a sound. Small. Warm in its own broken way.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Vyx looks back out at the road east. The pipeline. The drifting light.*

*Somewhere ahead: Consequence. Irongate. Kael.*

*Somewhere behind: District 7. The workshop. The wall of names.*

*Somewhere unknown: Davan.*

*The aether light pulses gently in the dark. Patient. Waiting.*

*Like it always has been.*

---

## END OF SCENE — "HIS FATHER'S WORK"

---

> **NOTE:**
> This scene is the emotional midpoint of Act 2.
> Everything before it was running away or running toward.
> After this scene Vyx understands what he's actually carrying.
> Not just grief. Not just anger. Not just hope for his brother.
> His father's work. His father's belief that people deserved better.
> His father's faith that someone would find it and know what to do with it.
> That someone was Pip.
> And now it's Vyx.
> The weight of that is enormous.
> The weight of that is also the first thing in two years that feels like PURPOSE rather than survival.
> Handle this scene carefully. It earns everything that comes after it.
