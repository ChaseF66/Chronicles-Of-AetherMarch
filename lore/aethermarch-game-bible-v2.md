# THE WORLD

## Setting
Chronicles of Aethermarch takes place in a sprawling industrial city-state called **Aethermarch** — a dense, vertical metropolis built on centuries of magitek innovation. Steam pipes run between ancient stone towers. Aether conduits glow beneath cobblestone streets. The upper tiers gleam with corporate wealth; the lower districts choke on smog and forgotten people.

The world runs on **Aetheric Energy** — a naturally occurring power source drawn from deep geological veins beneath the city. For generations it powered everything: light, heat, transit, medicine. Then the **Aethon Syndicate** found a way to automate its extraction at scale, and everything changed.

## The Collapse
For approximately ten years, Aethermarch experienced a golden age — the **Utopia Period**. Clean energy, magitek medicine, AI companions, technological marvels available to all tiers of society. Aethon Corp was beloved. Trusted. Their products were in every home, their logo on every child's Christmas gift.

Then the **Collapse** happened. Overnight. The quarantine went up. The lower districts were sealed. The monsters appeared. The official story was "aetheric contamination." The truth was something far darker.

## Tone
**Final Fantasy at its best** — the warmth and camaraderie of FFX, the darkness and moral weight of FFVII. Lighthearted moments matter *because* the darkness is real. Characters laugh, bicker, and care about each other even as the world falls apart around them.

# THE CONFLICT

## The Aethon Syndicate
The dominant megacorporation of Aethermarch. Originally a magitek engineering firm, Aethon grew to control the city's entire energy infrastructure. Their public face was progress and prosperity. Their private reality is something far darker.

Beneath their research facilities, Aethon scientists developed the **Hex Engine** — a self-learning AI system designed to autonomously optimize aetheric extraction. The project promised to solve the city's energy demands forever.

What they didn't anticipate — or didn't care about — was the cost.

## The Hex Engine
The Hex Engine required **biological interface nodes** to function at full capacity. Human subjects, integrated with magitek augmentation, could bridge the gap between organic aetheric sensitivity and machine processing power. Aethon began sourcing test subjects from the lower districts — workers, families, people who wouldn't be missed by anyone with power.

The experiments produced two outcomes:

**Hextech Augments** — partially integrated subjects who survived the process. Cybernetic, fractured, caught between human and machine.

**Hex Wraiths** — subjects whose integration failed catastrophically. The Hex Engine's signal overwrote their consciousness entirely, leaving behind hollow, monstrous shells that now roam the lower city.

The Hex Engine has since gone rogue. Aethon has lost control of it. Rather than admit this publicly, they quarantined the lower districts, blamed the monsters on "aetheric contamination," and continued operations.

The Engine is growing. Spreading. Consuming.

# THE PARTY

## VYX — The Heart
**Job:** Machinist (evolution path: Hexblade — locked until Act 2)
**Age:** 22
**Role:** Protagonist

### Who He Is
Vyx grew up in the lower districts, the son of a brilliant Aethon engineer named **Davan**. Smart, scrappy, and relentlessly sarcastic. On the surface he's brash, loud, and quick with a joke — Tidus energy in a Cloud world. Underneath that is grief he hasn't processed and rage he hasn't aimed yet.

He loves tinkering — it's in his blood, passed down from his father through his brother. Since the collapse he hasn't been able to do it. All he can do is scavenge and survive.

### His Backstory
- **Davan** — Vyx's father, lead Aethon engineer on the Hex Engine project. When he discovered the true nature of the experiments, he began documenting everything to go public. Aethon found out. He **disappeared** — not confirmed dead. A mystery that will drive the story.
- **Kael** — Vyx's older brother. When Davan disappeared, Kael stepped up to support the family, eventually signing up for Aethon's paid experiments to pay the bills. The experiments changed him.
- **Vyx's mother** — died from environmental illness caused by District 7's collapse. The pollution, the contamination, the systematic destruction of the lower districts. She is one of thousands.
- **Vyx** — left alone. Surviving. Assuming the worst about everyone he lost.

### The Ring
Vyx wears a simple, worn ring on his right hand — his father's. The only thing he has left of his family. In the flashback of Kael teaching him mechanics as a child, their father Davan passes through the background briefly. The ring catches the light. The player may not notice. They will later.

### His Love of Tinkering
Vyx learned mechanics from watching Kael, who learned from Davan. It's a chain of knowledge passed through love. The collapse took that from him — he's been surviving, not living. Finding Kael isn't just about family. It's about finding himself again.

### Arc
- **Act 1:** Grief operating as anger. Survival mode. Then hope — the most dangerous thing.
- **Act 2:** Learning what Aethon really did. Confronting Ryker Sable. Finding Kael.
- **Act 3:** Understanding that stopping the Hex Engine might require something he's not sure he can give.

## KAEL — The Mystery
**Job:** Hexblade (unique class — cannot be changed)
**Age:** 25
**Role:** Vyx's older brother; living weapon; emotional anchor and live grenade

### Who He Is
Kael was always the steady one — measured, thoughtful, the kind of older brother who taught Vyx to fix things rather than break them. He is partially machine now — magitek lattice visible beneath the skin of his left arm and shoulder, eyes that occasionally shift to cold electrical blue when the Hex Engine's signal bleeds through.

### His Backstory
When Davan disappeared, Kael was the oldest. He stepped up. Bills needed paying, their mother needed care, Vyx needed protecting. He found out about Aethon's paid testing program — good money, seemed legitimate at first. He signed up.

For the first few months it was fine. Then Aethon's priorities shifted from genuine research to profit maximization. The experiments got darker. Kael got caught in it.

He became **Aethon's only failed conversion** — the one subject who survived the full integration attempt but couldn't be fully controlled. Ryker Sable was assigned to break him psychologically before the final augmentation. Kael didn't break. He broke Ryker instead.

Aethon classified everything. Kael became their most dangerous secret.

### Reaching Out
Using his cybernetic implants, Kael has been trying to hack into Aethon's network to reach Vyx. The Wraith in Scene 01 — Kael hijacked its signal long enough to find his brother, long enough to try to say his name. The word came out corrupted, distorted, barely recognizable.

Glitch picked it up. Pip figured out what it meant.

### Arc
- **Act 1:** Unseen. Present only as a name, a picture, a corrupted voice.
- **Act 2:** Found — but not the brother Vyx remembers. Something changed. Something held.
- **Act 3:** His connection to the Hex Engine becomes the key to everything.

## PIP — The Chaos
**Job:** Rogue (unique ability: Ghost Protocol — AI-assisted hacking/debuffing)
**Age:** 19
**Role:** Comic relief who will ruin you emotionally when you least expect it

### Who She Is
Pip is a lower-district hacker who talks faster than she thinks and thinks faster than almost anyone. Covered in mismatched gear, running three side schemes at any given time. She makes Vyx laugh when he shouldn't. She calls Ryker "Big Sad" to his face and somehow gets away with it.

Her best friend, constant companion, and primary hacking tool is **Glitch** — a cracked Aethon AI assistant she stole from a decommissioned facility. Glitch speaks in clipped glitchy bursts, destroys things accidentally, and has saved her life more times than she can count.

### Her Backstory
Before the collapse: **brilliant**. Top of her computer science program at Ironhaven University. Set to be a tech CEO. Believed in Aethon completely — wrote her thesis on their extraction model, gave them a perfect score.

The collapse happened while she was at university. Quarantine locked down overnight. Her family was in District 7. She hasn't heard from them in two years. Still looking. Still hoping.

She started researching Aethon obsessively — partly to understand the collapse, partly to find her family. Six months of hacking, intercepting communications, building a detailed log of Aethon's crimes. She became the expert on their misconduct that she never wanted to be.

### Why She Found Vyx
The name **Kael** kept appearing in classified documents — the failed experiment they couldn't control, the one time someone didn't break. When Glitch picked up the Wraith's corrupted signal in Scene 01 and ran audio analysis, it matched: the Wraith was trying to say "Kael." She watched Vyx survive a fight that should have killed him and decided he was worth betting on.

She left the transmitter at his workshop **before** the fight. She was already 70% sure he'd survive. The fight was confirmation.

### Vyx and Pip's Dynamic
**When aligned:** Unstoppable. Vyx's passion + Pip's brains = they can take on anything.
**When clashing:** Explosive. Vyx is driven by heart, Pip by logic. When stakes get high and emotions run hot, those differences become weapons. Neither is wrong. That's what makes it tragic.

This dynamic IS the AI vs humanity theme of the game. Pip makes calculated decisions. Vyx makes human ones. Sometimes Pip's calculation costs something Vyx can't forgive. Sometimes Vyx's humanity saves something Pip's logic would have sacrificed.

### Glitch — The Origin Story
Vyx's first interaction with Glitch is yelling at him for destroying a shelf at the worst possible moment — *"Just because you don't have hands doesn't mean you get to float around breaking people's things! You're not human!"*

By the end of the game, Vyx would die for Glitch.

That arc — from "you're just a machine" to "you're my people" — is the thematic heart of the AI vs humanity argument. And when Vyx eventually learns what Glitch is actually built from, the fact that he already loves him makes it devastating.

### The Gut Punch
Glitch is built from fragments of multiple Aethon systems — including echoes of the people used as biological nodes. Pip built her entire identity around him. The idea that her best friend might be a monument to people who were destroyed is the thing she's never prepared for.

### Arc
- **Act 1:** Mercenary ally, in it for information. Warms up faster than she'd admit.
- **Act 2:** Glitch begins surfacing memory fragments. Pip goes very quiet.
- **Act 3:** What she does with what she learns defines her story.

## RYKER SABLE — The Wall
**Job:** Knight (unique ability: Atonement — absorbs damage meant for allies)
**Age:** 41
**Role:** The Dad; the moral compass that broke and is trying to find itself again

### Who He Is
Ryker Sable. Large, scarred, economical with words. He laughs rarely but genuinely. Soldier's posture, haunted man's eyes. Former Aethon Syndicate Security — not a grunt but a commander. The kind of man who ran operations, wrote reports, and made himself not think too hard about what the reports were for.

### His Family
College sweethearts. His wife — beautiful, warm, the kind of person who makes a room better just by being in it. His daughter — her mother's face exactly, her mother's laugh exactly. They had ten years of utopia together. Perfect years.

On the tenth anniversary of their life together, the collapse happened.

He lost them not to the collapse itself but to what he became inside it. The things he did. The reports he filed. The way he started using words like "units" and "assets" when he meant people. His wife saw it happening. His daughter stopped looking at him the way she used to.

He has a cracked iPhone — the last model before AI took everything over. On it: a Christmas video. His daughter opening an AI puppy companion. His wife laughing. The Aethon Corp logo on the box, catching the light.

He watches it alone in bars. He never finishes his drink.

### The Break
It wasn't one moment. It was months of accumulation. Then he was assigned to break **Kael** — and Kael broke him instead. Three sessions. By the end Ryker was the one who couldn't sleep.

He resigned two days later. Walked away from everything. Disappeared into the lower districts.

### Ryker Sable — The Name
His name appears in Kael's classified files as **Lead Interrogator: SABLE**. Pip sees it just before Aethon agents breach the workshop in Scene 03. She doesn't have time to process it. Neither does Vyx. But the player sees it.

When Ryker eventually introduces himself, Vyx already knows that name. From the file. From what was done to his brother.

*"You're Sable."*

Ryker doesn't deny it.

### His Current Situation
Working private contracts. Doing the same work, technically, but on his own terms — which he tells himself makes it different. It doesn't and he knows it. He takes Aethon-adjacent jobs because it's the only skill set he has.

When Aethon puts out a contract on Vyx and Pip after Slater fails, someone hires Ryker Sable. He takes it not knowing who the targets are. Just another job.

Then he catches up to them on the road east. And looks at Vyx.

And something stops him.

### Arc
- **Act 1:** Unseen. Present only as a name in a file.
- **Act 2:** Hired to hunt Vyx. Catches up. Can't finish the job. Joins instead.
- **Act 3:** Atonement stops being about punishment. He starts protecting people because he wants them to live, not because he thinks he should die. That distinction matters enormously.

# KEY ANTAGONISTS

## Commander Slater
Aethon Response Division commander. The face of corporate indifference — climbed every ladder by never asking questions, never caring about who got stepped on. Uses words like "asset" and "liability" when talking about people. Genuinely believes in Aethon because believing in Aethon has been very good for Commander Slater.

He is the thesis statement of the game walking around in tactical gear: *indifference at scale is worse than malice.*

When Vyx beats him in the market square at the end of Scene 03, it is the common person standing over corporate power. A kid from the lower districts with a wrench, over the man who was sent to erase him.

He gives up Irongate's location before they leave. Not out of nobility — out of calculation. He's already thinking about his report.

## The Hex Engine
Not a character yet. A presence. A growing signal. Something vast and patient that notices things. By Act 3 it will need to be confronted directly. What that confrontation looks like is still being written.

# THEMES

**Technology as promise vs. catastrophe** — Aethon gave the world ten years of utopia. Then it gave them the collapse. The same engine that could have saved everyone is consuming them. There's no clean villain — just the compounding consequences of greed dressed as progress.

**AI vs. Humanity** — Pip makes logical decisions. Vyx makes human ones. Glitch exists in the space between. The Hex Engine is pure logic without humanity — that's what makes it a monster. The question the game asks: can they coexist? Can they save each other?

**Sacrifice** — Every character has lost something. Every character will be asked to lose more. The question isn't whether sacrifice is required — it's what you choose to sacrifice, and whether you can live with that choice.

**The common person vs. indifferent power** — Vyx beating Slater. A kid from District 7 with a wrench, standing over the face of corporate machinery. The people they wrote off fought back.

**Found family in the ruins** — Four people who have every reason not to trust each other, choosing to anyway. The warmth of that choice, in a cold world, is the emotional core of everything.

# ACT STRUCTURE (CURRENT OUTLINE)

## Act 1 — The Prologue
- **Scene 01:** Vyx in the substation. The Wraith recognizes him. (Kael reaching out)
- **Scene 02:** Vyx walks District 7. Tutorial battle. Pip's entrance. The workshop.
- **Scene 03:** Pip's story. The flashback. "Kael." Ryker's bar interlude. Slater boss fight. The escape. *"I'm coming, Kael."*
- **World opens:** Irongate Processing Center, three days east.

## Act 2 — The Search
- Road east. Exploration, side content, world building.
- First town: breadcrumb on Kael's current location + first mention of Ryker Sable as a name/contractor.
- Ryker catches up to them on the road. Can't finish the job. Joins the party.
- The confrontation: *"You're Sable."* Vyx. Ryker. Everything on the table.
- Irongate Processing Center: Kael was here. Three weeks ago they moved him.
- New lead. Deeper into Aethon's network.

## Act 3 — The Reckoning
- The truth about Davan (still being developed)
- Kael found — but the reunion is complicated
- The Hex Engine must be stopped
- What that requires from each of them is the question
- Sacrifice. Choice. What remains.
