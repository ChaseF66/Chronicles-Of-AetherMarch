# THE FRAME

## The Room

*Sometime in the future.*
*The world is different. Not perfect — never perfect —
but different. Better. The air is cleaner.
The infrastructure works. The hierarchy that defined
everything before the road east has been dismantled,
rebuilt, argued over, and rebuilt again.*

*A room. Warm. Lived in.*

*PIP is in the background.*
*At a desk. Coding. Still building. Still fixing.
Because that's who she is —
she never stopped.
Not even now. Not even after everything.*

*VYX is older.*
*The wrench is still there — different now, worn differently,
but still there. The ring still on his hand.
His father's ring. His anchor. His light.*

*And in front of him —*

*A child. Or grandchild.*
*Young enough to not know the world before.
Young enough to have grown up in the one that came after.
Young enough to think clean air is just what air is.*

*They've been asking questions.*
*The way children ask questions about before —
not quite believing it was ever that bad,
not quite able to picture the people
their parents or grandparents used to be.*

*Vyx looks at them for a moment.*

*Then he starts talking.*

# THE CHRONICLES

## What They Are

The Chronicles of Aethermarch are Glitch's archive.

Every `[GLITCH — FILING]` entry in every scene.
Every scan. Every classification. Every HUMAN — UNCLASSIFIED — SIGNIFICANT.
Every slag heap. Every corrupted drone. Every star through the smog gap.
Every moment he watched Vyx look east and filed it under ONGOING — PRIORITY — WATCH.
Every entry in WYRM — ONGOING — STATUS: FRIEND.

Built from Day One of the road east.
Built without anyone asking.
Built because that's what he was made to do —
even if nobody knew what it was for yet.

The player experiences the Chronicles as comedy.
Glitch being annoying. Getting in the way. Scanning irrelevant things.
Beeping at slag heaps. Filing things nobody asked him to file.

Then the reveal comes.

And everything recontextualizes.

Every laugh becomes something else.
Every "GLITCH." becomes something else.
Every scan becomes an entry in the record
of how a world was rebuilt.

He was never malfunctioning.
He was documenting.
He was always documenting.
That's what he was built for.
Even Pip didn't know.

---

## Why The Title Is Perfect

*Chronicles* — from the Greek *khronikos* — of time. A record of events in order.

The Chronicles of Aethermarch are not named for a war.
Not named for a kingdom.
Not named for a hero.

Named for the record.
Named for the archivist.
Named for the idea that someone was paying attention
the whole time
even when nobody noticed.

A broken little drone
built by a girl who couldn't leave a stuffed animal behind
filing everything
because nobody told him not to.

That's the title.
That's always been the title.
Now we know why.

# THE TAGLINE — FULL MEANING

*"Aethermarch doesn't make heroes. It makes us."*

## What It Meant Before

A rallying cry. A statement about found family.
Four people who had every reason not to trust each other —
choosing to anyway.

## What It Means Now

Vyx saying it to his child.
Looking back at everything.
At Pip in her office.
At what they built together.
At who they were when they started —

Young. Scared. Running.
A mechanic who kept losing his socket wrench.
A girl with a stuffed aetherwyrm in the bottom of her pack.
A drone nobody took seriously.

*"We weren't heroes.*
*We were just people.*
*And we built this anyway."*

That's the whole game in one sentence.
It was always the whole game in one sentence.
Now it carries the full weight of everything that earned it.

# VYX AS NARRATOR

## What This Means Structurally

Every scene in the game is Vyx telling this story.
Which means:

- He already knows how it ends.
- He chooses what to tell and what to linger on.
- The things he lingers on are the things that mattered most.
- The small moments — the socket wrench, the rations,
  the slag heap, the stars — he remembered those.
  He remembered those above everything else.
  That tells you something about who he is.

## What This Means Emotionally

There are moments in the game that are painful.
Losses. Costs. Things that can't be taken back.

Vyx lived through all of them.
And he's still here.
Telling the story.
In a warm room.
With Pip in the background.
Still coding.

That's not a spoiler.
That's a promise.

The player knows from the first frame
that Vyx makes it.
That Pip makes it.
That something worth telling a child about
came out the other side of everything.

The question is what it cost.
The question is who else made it.
The question is what the world looks like now.

Those questions carry the whole game.

## His Voice

Older but not old.
Warm but not soft.
Someone who has processed the grief
and kept the love
and learned the difference between the two.

He doesn't dramatize.
He doesn't perform.
He just tells it the way it happened —
the small moments alongside the big ones,
the funny parts alongside the devastating ones,
because that's what it actually was.

That's what life actually is.

---

# PIP IN THE BACKGROUND

She doesn't stop coding.

Not because she doesn't care —
because she's heard this story before.
She knows every beat.
She was there for every beat.

But she lets him tell it.
Because the telling matters.
Because the child needs to hear it from him.
Because some things need to be passed down
in the specific voice of the person who lived them.

Occasionally she corrects him.
Without looking up.

[PIP — from the background, not looking up]*
*"That's not how it happened."*

[VYX]*
*"That's how I remember it."*

[PIP]*
*"You're misremembering."*

[VYX — to the child, completely unbothered]*
*"She does this."*

Because that's what he was built for.

Even if nobody knew it yet.
