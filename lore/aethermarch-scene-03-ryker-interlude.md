## INTERLUDE: THE VIDEO

---

*(hard cut)*

---

[SCENE]
*A bar. But calling it that is generous.*

*Low ceilings, lower light. The kind of place that stopped pretending to be anything a long time ago. Mismatched stools, a counter worn smooth by years of elbows and regret. The smell of cheap aether spirits and something older underneath — wood, smoke, the particular exhaustion of a room that has absorbed too many quiet nights.*

*It's almost empty. The bartender doesn't look up.*

[SOUND]
*Ambient bar noise. Low. Distant. The creak of the building settling. Outside — the mid-districts, quieter than District 7 but never quite silent.*

[SCENE]
*At the far end of the bar: a man.*

*Large. Scarred in the specific way that means a life, not an accident. The kind of stillness that only comes from someone who spent years learning to be very, very good at waiting. His drink is in front of him — something amber, something strong. He hasn't touched it.*

*He's looking at something in his hand.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*A phone. Old. Cracked screen, edges worn smooth from years of handling. The kind of device the older generation kept when everything went digital and then went further and then went somewhere nobody asked for. From a simpler time. From before.*

*On the cracked screen — a video. Playing on loop.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE — THE VIDEO]
*Christmas morning. Warm light. A living room that was loved — not decorated, LOVED, the difference visible in every detail. Ornaments that don't match. A tree slightly crooked. The comfortable chaos of a family that doesn't perform happiness, just has it.*

*A WOMAN — beautiful in the way that has nothing to do with trying. College sweetheart written in every comfortable movement, in the way she laughs at something off-screen like she's been laughing at that same thing for twenty years and it's still funny.*

*A GIRL — maybe eight, nine. Her mother's face exactly, her mother's laugh exactly, her mother's everything. The kind of child who makes you understand why people use the word joy.*

*She tears into a present. Ribbons everywhere. She pulls out a box.*

*Inside: an AI companion. A small, perfect, luminous construct in the shape of a puppy — soft light, responsive eyes, the cutting edge of the utopia made small enough to fit in a child's arms. She SCREAMS with delight. Holds it up.*

*On the side of the box, catching the Christmas light for just a moment:*

*AETHON CORP. — "Building Tomorrow, Together."*

*The girl doesn't notice. She's already holding the puppy to her chest, already naming it, already in love.*

*Her mother looks toward the camera — toward him — and her expression is everything. Pride and love and the particular happiness of watching your child be completely, purely happy.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*The video plays. The girl laughs. The woman looks toward the camera with everything in her eyes.*

*The camera begins to pull back. Slowly. Deliberately.*

*We see the phone first — that cracked screen, that warm light bleeding out into the cold bar around it. Then the hand holding it. Scarred. Still. Unmoving.*

*The camera keeps pulling.*

*We see the drink. Untouched. The condensation ring beneath it. The bar worn smooth by years of elbows and regret.*

*The camera keeps pulling.*

*And then — his face.*

*(hard cut from the video's warmth to his face)*

[SCENE]
*RYKER.*

*The video still playing in his hand, its warm light flickering across features that have seen too much and forgotten none of it. The contrast is devastating — that small bright rectangle of everything he had, held in hands that did things he can't take back.*

*His face.*

*Not angry. Not numb. Something worse than both — completely, quietly aware. Every line of it carrying the specific weight of a man who knows exactly what he lost and exactly why he lost it and has made peace with neither.*

*The woman on the screen laughs at something.*

*He doesn't.*

*The girl holds up her Aethon puppy. Beaming.*

*His jaw tightens. Just slightly. Just once.*

*The video loops back to the beginning.*

*(beat — long, held)*

*He watches it start again.*

*(beat)*

[NARRATION]
*His name is RYKER.*
*He used to be someone important.*
*He hasn't decided yet what he is now.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*His comm unit crackles. A message. Encrypted — the kind that means private work, unofficial channels, the economy that exists in the space between what the Syndicate controls and what it doesn't.*

*A job offer. Details fragmented, deliberately vague. But the location is clear.*

*Lower districts. District 7.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*Ryker looks at the comm. Then back at the phone. The video has reached the moment where his daughter holds up the Aethon puppy again.*

*He watches it for three more seconds.*

*Then he closes it.*

*Puts the phone in his chest pocket. Over his heart. Where it always goes.*

*(beat)*

*He picks up his drink.*

*Takes one long pull.*

*Sets it back down.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*He stands. Pulls on his coat — heavy, practical, the coat of someone who has learned that weather is the least of what you need to be prepared for. He drops payment on the bar without counting it.*

*The bartender still doesn't look up.*

*Ryker walks toward the door. Stops with his hand on the frame.*

*(beat)*

[SCENE]
*He doesn't look back at the bar.*
*He doesn't look back at the drink.*
*He steps out into the mid-district night.*

*Somewhere across the city, in a workshop in District 7, a door is coming off its hinges.*

*He doesn't know that yet.*

*He will.*

---

*(hard cut back to the workshop)*

---

*The door comes off its hinges.*

*(hard cut to the chase — already running)*
