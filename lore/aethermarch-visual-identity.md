# CHRONICLES OF AETHERMARCH
## Visual Document — Character Identity Colors & Flow States
### Version 1.0

---

# OVERVIEW

Every character in Chronicles of Aethermarch has an **identity color palette** — a set of colors that runs through their costume, their accessories, their aura, their flow state, and their emotional arc. These colors are not random. They are WHO THEY ARE made visible.

The colors should appear:
- In their costume and gear design
- In their weapon or tool detailing
- In ambient lighting when they're the focus
- In their UI elements (HP bar color, name plate)
- In their flow state aura
- In subtle environmental details around them

When all four characters are in flow state simultaneously — if that moment ever comes — the combined visual of blue-green, black-red, grey-black and pink-green is the most visually striking moment in the game. It should feel earned. Because it is.

# VYX — BLUE & TOXIC GREEN

## Identity Colors
**Primary:** Ethereal blue
**Secondary:** Toxic green
**Accent:** Faint gold (inherited from Davan — visible in the ring)

## What These Colors Mean
Blue is raw aether. The natural frequency. The thing Vyx was born to access.
Toxic green is District 7. Two years of contaminated air, corrupted aether, a poisoned world seeping into his biology.

Together they represent: **natural power flowing through an unnatural world.** Beautiful and wrong simultaneously. The most honest visual representation of what Vyx IS — something pure trying to exist in something broken.

## In His Costume
- Patched work jacket — primarily dark and worn, but the patch materials have blue-green tints. Salvaged aether-adjacent materials absorbed color from proximity to conduits.
- Tool loops on the jacket — wrapped in blue-green cord, salvaged from pipeline insulation
- Wrench handle — wrapped in the same cord. His most important tool marked with his color.
- Boots — dark, practical, but the soles have that faint blue glow from walking District 7's aether-saturated cobblestones for two years
- His father's ring — gold. The only warm color on him. Stands out.
- Eyes — even before flow state, catch the light slightly blue. Always have. He never noticed.

## In His Aura — Flow State
**Trigger visual:** Black and white burst frame — one second of total overload
**Active aura:** Ethereal blue bleeding into toxic green. Not clean. Not pure. The color of raw aether filtered through a body that's been living in poison. It PULSES — not steady, not smooth. Like a signal with interference.
**Eyes:** Blue-green glow. Faint. Like the pipeline bleeds.
**Movement:** Leaves afterimages in the same blue-green as he moves
**Crash:** Colors fade from outside in — the green goes first, then the blue, until he's back to normal and on one knee

## Arc Color Note
As Vyx's story progresses and he understands more of what he is, the toxic green in his aura begins to CLARIFY. The poison bleeding out of it. By Act 3 his flow state is cleaner — still blue-green but the green shifts warmer. Less toxic. More vital.
The world is still broken. But he's becoming something that exists BEYOND the brokenness rather than through it.

# KAEL — BLACK & RED (TRANSFORMING)

## Identity Colors
**Primary:** Deep black
**Secondary:** Corrupted red
**Accent:** Cold electrical blue (the Hex Engine bleeding through)
**Post-arc:** [UNDOCUMENTED — this is the reveal]

## What These Colors Mean
Black is what Aethon made of him. The absence of what he was supposed to be.
Red is the corruption — the Hex Engine's signal forced into his biology, turned violent by his frequency's resistance.
Cold blue is the Hex Engine itself bleeding through his implants when his control slips.

Together they represent: **power that was forced, that fought back, that is still fighting.** Not chosen. Survived. The colors of someone who went through fire and came out the other side changed in ways that are both terrible and extraordinary.

## In His Costume
- Dark clothing. Practical. No ornamentation except what Aethon put in him.
- Magitek lattice visible beneath the skin of his left arm and shoulder — glows faint red when stressed, cold blue when the Hex Engine bleeds through
- His eyes shift — brown normally, cold electrical blue when the Engine's signal surfaces
- The red in his lattice is most visible at the joints — the seams between human and machine
- He wears long sleeves when he can. Not from shame. From not wanting people to flinch.
- After his arc begins resolving: the red in the lattice starts changing. The first sign before anything else changes.

## In His Aura — Flow State
**Trigger visual:** No burst frame — his flow state doesn't announce itself. It ARRIVES. One moment he's fighting. The next the air around him is different.
**Active aura:** Deep black with red crackling through it like lightning in storm clouds. The Hex Engine's own signal turned against it — he's using the frequency they forced into him as a weapon against the system that forced it.
**Eyes:** Full electrical blue during flow state. Not faint. Complete.
**Movement:** Doesn't leave afterimages — leaves ABSENCE. The space where he was goes slightly darker for a half-second after he moves.
**Sound:** A crackling static underlies everything he does in flow state. The Hex Engine's signal. His signal. Indistinguishable now.
**Crash:** The red dims. The black remains. He doesn't go to one knee — he goes very still. Like a machine cycling down. It takes a moment before he moves again.

## Post-Arc Color
Undocumented here deliberately. The transformation of his aura colors after his arc completes is one of the most important visual moments in the game. It will be documented when the arc is written. What we know: it involves neither black nor red. It is something that has never appeared in Aethon's systems. Something new.

# RYKER SABLE — GREY & BLACK

## Identity Colors
**Primary:** Weathered grey
**Secondary:** Deep black
**Accent:** Faint warm amber — almost invisible, almost gone. Almost.

## What These Colors Mean
Grey is endurance. Everything worn down to its essential nature. Not darkness — grey isn't the absence of light, it's light that's been through too much.
Black is weight. The specific gravity of a man who has been carrying something too heavy for too long.
The faint amber — almost invisible in his early appearances — is what remains of who he was before. The man in the video. It grows across the game. Slowly. Undramatically. The way real change happens.

## In His Costume
- Heavy coat — grey, practical, the kind that's been through weather and worse. Multiple pockets. Everything has a purpose.
- Armor underneath — black, matte, no shine. Doesn't want to be seen. Doesn't want to be noticed. Just wants to be useful.
- Scarring visible on his hands and neck — grey-silver against his skin, the specific texture of old wounds
- His weapon — whatever it is, it's grey and black. No decoration. Nothing extra.
- His flask — the one constant. Simple metal. Grey.
- The amber detail: nearly invisible early. A worn strap. A faded patch. Something that used to be brighter. By Act 3 it's more visible. He stopped hiding it.

## In His Aura — Flow State
**Trigger:** His flow state cannot trigger while he believes he's absorbing damage as punishment. It can ONLY trigger when he takes a hit meant for someone else because he wants them to live. That distinction is the unlock condition. Mechanically and narratively.
**Trigger visual:** No burst. No flash. The air around him goes HEAVY. Like gravity increased. Like the space around him decided to take things seriously.
**Active aura:** Grey-black. Not glowing — ABSORBING. Where the others radiate outward, Ryker's state pulls inward. He becomes a gravity well. Attacks aimed at allies near him curve toward him instead. He takes them. Every one.
**Eyes:** Don't glow. Go very still. The haunted quality disappears — replaced by complete, absolute presence. He's HERE. Fully. For the first time.
**Movement:** Slow. Deliberate. Immovable. He doesn't dodge. He doesn't need to. Everything hits him and he remains standing.
**Crash:** Doesn't crash the way the others do. He just... stops absorbing. Returns to normal. Looks at whoever he protected. Doesn't say anything.
**The amber during flow state:** Visible. Briefly. In the grey-black aura, a thread of warm amber running through it. Gone when flow state ends. But it was there.

## Arc Color Note
By Act 3, the amber in Ryker's palette is undeniable. It doesn't replace the grey and black — those are still there, still HIM. But the amber runs through them now. Warm. Real. The man in the video and the man he became, existing in the same color space for the first time.

# PIP — PINK & GREEN

## Identity Colors
**Primary:** Warm pink
**Secondary:** Electric green
**Accent:** Glitch's colors — because they've always matched. Neither of them planned it.

## What These Colors Mean
Pink is warmth. Connection. The thing Pip pretends she doesn't need and absolutely does. The color of someone whose natural state is caring even when caring has cost her everything.
Green is growth. Adaptability. The hacker's color — finding the path through any system, growing into any gap. Also life. Also hope. The thing that keeps going even in contaminated soil.

Together they represent: **chaotic, alive, impossible to contain.** The most colorful character in a muted world. Not because she's naive — because she CHOOSES color as an act of defiance. In a world that wants her grey and compliant, she is PINK AND GREEN and loud about it.

## In Her Costume
- Mismatched gear that somehow works — pink cable management, green lens goggles
- Every piece salvaged or stolen but color-coordinated in a way that can't be accidental
- Tool pouches in pink, wiring in green, the chaos organized by her own internal system
- Glitch's casing — cracked pink shell, green flickering eye. They match. They always did. Neither of them chose it. It just is.
- Her hands — stained with the particular combination of aether residue and tech grease that leaves green-pink tints on her fingertips
- When she's deep in focus, working on Glitch or hacking a system, her goggles glow green

## In Her Aura — Flow State
**Trigger:** Cannot trigger alone. Pip's flow state activates when she TRUSTS completely — when she stops calculating outcomes and commits to the team. It's a relational trigger. The most Pip thing possible.
**Trigger visual:** Glitch pulses first — his eye going from flickering to solid, brilliant green. Then Pip's aura ignites around BOTH of them simultaneously.
**Active aura:** Pink and green — warm and electric, chaotic and precise. It spreads between her and Glitch like they're one system. Because in this state, they are. Her mind and his processing unified. She thinks it and he executes it before the thought is finished. He detects it and she responds before he reports it.
**Eyes:** Green — the same green as Glitch's eye. Synchronized.
**Movement:** Unpredictable. Chaotic on purpose. She moves in patterns that can't be predicted because she's not following logic — she's following INTUITION backed by Glitch's processing. The combination is genuinely uncalculable.
**Crash:** Glitch dims first. Then her aura fades. She always checks on Glitch immediately after. Every time. Without exception.
**Sound:** Glitch's beeping becomes musical during her flow state. Almost. A rhythm that shouldn't work but does. Like her.

## Arc Color Note
Pip's colors don't change across her arc. She doesn't need to transform — she needs to be SEEN. The journey is other people learning to see what was always there. By Act 3, when the party is fully formed and trusting each other, Pip's pink and green stops looking out of place in the muted world. It starts looking like exactly what the world needed.

# THE PARTY TOGETHER

## Color Relationships
**Vyx (blue-green) and Kael (black-red):** Brothers. Complementary. Cold light and hot darkness. What they share: the frequency. What differs: one is raw, one is forced. Their auras, when active simultaneously, create a visual tension that resolves into something neither produces alone.

**Pip (pink-green) and Ryker (grey-black):** The most contrasting pair. Warm chaos and cold weight. Her colors make his look darker by comparison. His colors make hers look brighter. They are the emotional poles of the party — and the relationship between them (her calling him Big Sad, him laughing for the first time) is the warmth at the center of everything.

**Vyx (blue-green) and Pip (pink-green):** Share the green. Not the same green — his is toxic, hers is electric. But it's there. A connection they don't know they have until someone points it out. The green is life insisting on itself in a world that makes that hard.

**Kael (black-red) and Ryker (grey-black):** The darkest pair. The most loaded. The man who was broken and the man who broke him, sharing a color space that is all shadow. Their reconciliation — when it comes — is the most visually subtle moment in the game. Nothing changes in their colors. But the way they occupy the same space changes entirely.

## When All Four Flow States Are Active
If this moment occurs — and it should occur exactly once, at the most important moment in Act 3 — the combined visual is:

Blue-green. Black-red. Grey-black. Pink-green.

Cold and hot. Dark and bright. Heavy and chaotic. The full spectrum of what this party IS, all at once, all together.

It should feel like the first time.

It should feel like everything they've been through was leading here.

It should feel like the world has never seen anything like this before.

Because it hasn't.
